# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick people into transferring money through digital payment apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. As online payments become more popular among college students and young professionals, cybercriminals increasingly target these users.

A typical UPI fraud usually happens in several steps. First, the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media platform and pretends to be a bank representative, customer support agent, or buyer or seller in an online marketplace. Next, the scammer sends a fake payment request or asks the victim to scan a QR code. They often claim that the victim will receive money after approving the request. However, in reality, approving the request actually sends money from the victim’s account to the scammer. In some cases, scammers also ask for OTPs or personal banking details to gain access to the victim’s account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are common targets. Many victims lose significant amounts of money because they do not realize the fraud until it is too late.

The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal information. Victims may also face difficulty recovering their money. To prevent such crimes, users should never approve unknown payment requests, avoid sharing OTPs, and report fraud immediately through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or helpline number 1930.
